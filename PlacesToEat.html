Peter Street Kitchen

Winner of 'Best Luxury Restaurant’ in the UK at the British Restaurant Awards 2019, Peter Street Kitchen offers an exquisite selection of contemporary Japanese and Mexican small plates, complemented with the finest wines, sake and our signature cocktails in the heart of Manchester.

Salford Museum & Art Gallery Cafe

Situated within the proximity of the Salford Museum and Art Gallery, the cafe offers a selection of delicious sandwiches, homemade scones, cakes and pastries, served with loose leaf tea or fresh ground coffee for the afternoon tea.

Three Little Words

Sip gorgeous cocktails, G&Ts, beers, and wines in beautifully restored Grade-II* listed red brick arches in the heart of Manchester. Home to Three Little Words and The Spirit of Manchester Distillery, the arches beneath Manchester Central have been lovingly opened up and turned into a stunning distillery, bar and restaurant. Drink. Dine. Distil.

Places to Eat

From 3-star Michelin gourmet restaurants, family restaurants, to cafes and bars, Manchester has a diverse culinary scene for different people with different tastes.

Restaurants

From Michelin 3-star gourmet restaurants serving European dishes, family eateries that serves traditional British foods to vegan friendly cafes, Manchester's repuration for great food and drink will satisfy everyone's tastebuds.

Store Street Exchange

Store Street Exchange restaurant and Store Street Craft Bar offers a friendly and unique dining experience, where simple dishes are served with an exciting twist. The menu offers everything from burgers to steaks, salads to fish, with the signature being hot chicken straight from the rotisserie served whole or half with your choice your side.

Writght's Fish and Chips

Located only one block away from the John Rylands Library, this often jam-packed place is a great place to enjoy some generous portions of fish and chips in the city.


Let's Go!
Randall & Aubin

Acclaimed Anglo-French brasseriewas established as a butcher’s shop in 1911 selling only the best produce, very quickly becoming one of the finest butchers in town. For the past 20 years, the celebrated and much loved restaurant serves the freshest seafood, mouthwatering and succulent rotisserie meats with premium ingredients from carefully chosen and local suppliers.

Pubs & Bars

Bars & Pubs are pretty much an integral part of the British culture, and Manchester has a diverse selection of bars and pubs that will satisfy your desire of some nightlife excitement, whether you want to sit down and get some beer with your friends, or get pumped up for an live event.

The Deaf Institute

The Deaf Institute is an independent fun loving bar and venue, spread across 3 floors, with 3 bars, a kitchen and a roof terrace that is housed in a historic Manchester building originally built in 1878 as its namesake, and was later refurbished to its former glory. The main bar have big sharing tables and leather booths, expect fresh and tasty food, a sweetie shop inspired cocktail list, frozen cocktails, local ales and craft beers served up to a rock ’n roll sound track.

Refuge by Volta

With its welcome ambiance and sociable buzz, The Refuge has become a hotspot where you can play all day and stay all night. Sip cocktails, whizz through work emails, catch a live DJ, sample pours from a local brewery: You can do it all and more.

Black Dog Ballroom

A slice of NYC’s secret downtown speakeasy style located in Manchester’s Northern Quarter in the form of the Blackdog Ballroom. Offering gourmet burgers and pizzas, draught beers, cocktails, pool tables and a secret room, the stylish new bar is open daily from Midday to 4am with food available ‘til 1am

Cafes and Delis

Sit down and enjoy a cup of tea and coffee while you munch on your sandwich or bagel. There is no lacking in cafes and delis if you want to spend your afternoon relaxing in the city, or want to grab something light to eat as you tour around the city.

Cosy Club

Cosy Club aims to provide a little something for everyone, serving breakfast in the morning and staying open late for drinks in the evening. It’s the perfect venue for your dress-down Sundays or your glad-rag Friday nights, your lunchtime getaways or your family fun Saturday afternoons.


Let's Go!
The Great Hall Cafe

Every day, Head Chef Chris Watson-Gunby creates a menu inspired by the freshest seasonal ingredients, creating a wholesome range of refreshing salads, homemade sandwiches, tasty tagines and heart-warming stews. Pull up a sofa and escape the city crowds, indulge in a traditional Afternoon Tea or start the day with a coffee to go.


Let's Go!
Electrik

Independently-owned Electrik is a cafe by day and a bar by night, offering cask ales, premium imported beers and award winning wines, uniquely roasted coffee and a plethora of teas which can be enjoyed with the lovingly homemade cakes. Electrik also boasts an enviable jukebox, free WiFi, daily papers and book exchange. Little ones are also welcome.
