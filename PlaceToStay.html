Featured Hotels

Manchester is home of some of the finest hotels in varying locations, so you can better plan your trip to explore what this city has to offer.

The Edwardian Manchester

Following a recent multi-million-pound redesign, the Radisson Collection hotel has been transformed into a lifestyle destination that introduces a new standard of service. Its 263 bedrooms are distinguished by ample space and floor-to-ceiling windows, whilst many of the stunning suites feature sweeping city views, entertaining areas and outdoor space.

Hotel Football

Co-owned by some of the members of Manchester United's Class of '92 veteran players, Hotel Football is situated right opposite to Old Trafford Stadium, and it boasts numerous unique features to make it a go-to destination for fans, families, business users and tourists.

The Lowry Hotel

Positioned in the perfect location the hotel encompasses a stunning view of the river and Spinningfields to one side, and peers onto the evolving city of Salford on the other, the award-winning Lowry dominates the skyline with its curved, glass-fronted façade. Boasting 165 rooms and suites with floor to ceiling windows for gazing out onto stunning views.

Beds & Breakfast

Spend a night at a countryside English cottage for the authentic English countryside experience, or one of the chain establishments with good quality. Whatever your choice is, Manchester has a unique B&B accomodation.

Foxstone Edge Cottage

Weaver's Cottage is a semi-detached cottage on the owner's smallholding, close to Slaithwaite in West Yorkshire. The cottage sleeps four people in a super king-size double bedroom with an en-suite shower room and a sofa bed in the sitting room. With excellent accomodation including a well-equipped kitchen, sitting room, off-road parking and patio area with furnituremagnificent views all around, Weaver's Cottage is a superb location for a peaceful holiday in magnificent countryside.

Abbey Lodge

A comfortable Edwardian house located in a quiet residential suburb with large luxurious en suite rooms. Convenient for an overnight stop, short break or family accommodation. 10 minutes drive from the city centre, MEN arena, GMEX, Trafford Centre and the Lowry at Salford Quays. Chorlton provides a lively community atmosphere with restaurants and cafe bars offering something for everyone.

The Church View

Church View House offers unique, boutique-style bed and breakfast accommodation in the heart of Ramsbottom, just a 5 minute walk from the town centre. It offers 2 spacious suites on the second floor – The Attic and The Loft, each with a large luxury ensuite bathroom with walk-in showers and free-standing baths. The rooms have tea and coffee making facilities – kettle, fresh milk and a choice of teas, herbal teas and coffees and have flat screen TVs and wi-fi broadband internet.

Hostels

Backpacking or on a shoestring budget? No worries. Manchester also has a large number of quality hostels, perfectly situated to explore the city.

Manchester YHA

This Manchester Youth Hostel is within easy reach of some of the best shopping, sport, nightlife, museums and theatres in north of England. This canal-side backpackers hostel has been re-fitted, so a city break will be even more comfortable for a weekend break close to Manchester's city centre. Brilliant for a pre-dinner drink in the bar or a tasty evening meal. Or you can people-watch from the restaurant window or terrace.

Wilmslow Park

Wilmslow Park offers stylish and well equipped self-catering summer accommodation in Manchester with en-suite bedrooms with luxury facilities. Each apartment comprises of 4 to 5 bedrooms and each cluster flat contains a variety of different room types; singled bed en-suite and double bed en-suite. The flats have a shared lounge area, dining facilities and a shared kitchen.

Castleshaw Center

Castleshaw offers hostel-style accommodation for groups of up to 38 people in 9 bedrooms in a beautiful location on the edge of the Pennine Hills. We are close to the Pennine Bridleway, a mile from the Peak District National Park but only 14 miles from Manchester City centre. The dramatic landscape of the Peak District National Park and South Pennines provide the perfect backdrop for visitors to enjoy their stay.
